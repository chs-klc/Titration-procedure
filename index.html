Topic 4: Acids & Bases

Standard Solution Preparation

The Misconception

Many students believe that to prepare a \(1.0 \text{ M}\) solution, you simply dissolve \(1.0 \text{ mole}\) of solute into exactly \(1.0 \text{ dm}^3\) of water.

The Right Concept

Molarity is defined by the volume of the entire solution, not just the solvent!

$$\text{Molarity (M)} = \frac{\text{Moles of Solute (mol)}}{\text{Volume of Solution (dm}^3)}$$

When solute dissolves in water, the total volume increases. If you add solute to \(1.0 \text{ dm}^3\) of water, the final volume will be \(> 1.0 \text{ dm}^3\), resulting in a molarity lower than expected.

Correct Method: Add the solute to a volumetric flask, dissolve in a small amount of water, and then top up with water exactly to the graduation mark.

The Misconception

Students may forget to transfer the washings from the beaker, glass rod, and filter funnel to the volumetric flask.

Why Washings Matter

Some solute remains on the glassware after transfer. Without washing, you lose this solute, resulting in fewer moles in the final solution.

$$\text{Actual Molarity} = \frac{\text{Moles Transferred}}{\text{Volume}} < \text{Expected Molarity}$$

Correct Method: After transferring the solution, wash the beaker, glass rod, and filter funnel with distilled water several times. Pour all washings into the volumetric flask to ensure all solute is transferred.

The Misconception

Students may forget to rinse the pipette with the solution to be contained before use, leaving residual distilled water inside.

Why Rinsing Matters

A wet pipette contains residual distilled water that will dilute the solution during delivery, reducing its concentration.

$$\text{Water in pipette} + \text{Solution} = \text{Diluted Solution}$$

Correct Method: Wash the pipette first with distilled water, then rinse it 2-3 times with the solution to be contained. This removes residual water and ensures accurate concentration.

The Misconception

Students may forget to rinse the burette with the solution (e.g. solution A) before filling it, leaving residual distilled water inside.

Why Rinsing Matters

Residual water in the burette dilutes the titrant (solution A). This means more volume of solution A is needed to reach the end point, giving an inaccurate titre value.

$$\text{Diluted titrant} \Rightarrow \text{Larger titre} \Rightarrow \text{Wrong molarity of B}$$

Correct Method: After washing with distilled water, rinse the burette 2–3 times with solution A before filling. This ensures the concentration of the titrant is accurate.
